# Evidence Quality Rubric

## Purpose

This rubric classifies sources used in the scaffolding manuscript so that claims are supported with appropriately strong evidence. The project should avoid treating preprints, policy commentary, and empirical studies as interchangeable.

## Evidence Tiers

### Tier 1: Foundational Theory

Use for concepts that define the field or establish long-standing theoretical foundations.

Examples:

- Scaffolding theory
- Zone of proximal development
- Productive failure
- Self-regulated learning theory

Appropriate manuscript use:

- Definitions
- Conceptual framing
- Theoretical motivation

Limitations:

- Does not by itself establish effects for modern LLM systems.

### Tier 2: Peer-Reviewed Empirical Evidence

Use for claims about observed learning outcomes, scaffold effects, calibration, transfer, or learner behavior.

Appropriate manuscript use:

- Evidence that a design choice has been tested
- Outcome claims
- Comparisons across learning conditions

Limitations:

- Must be checked for population, task domain, sample size, and generalizability.

### Tier 3: Systematic Review or Meta-Analysis

Use for high-level synthesis about AI tutoring, programming education, adaptive learning, or scaffolding effects.

Appropriate manuscript use:

- State of the literature
- Research gaps
- General patterns across studies

Limitations:

- May lag behind fast-moving LLM research.

### Tier 4: Preprint or Emerging System Paper

Use for recent LLM-era methods, benchmarks, or system designs that have not yet completed peer review.

Appropriate manuscript use:

- Emerging research direction
- Design inspiration
- Recent technical comparison

Limitations:

- Do not use as sole support for strong empirical claims.
- Mark clearly as preprint if used in manuscript.

### Tier 5: Policy, Commentary, Survey, or Context Source

Use for motivation, stakeholder concerns, and applied relevance.

Appropriate manuscript use:

- Framing practical concern
- Describing institutional or educational context

Limitations:

- Should not support causal or empirical learning claims.

## Claim Support Rules

### Strong Claims

Examples:

- Adaptive fading improves transfer.
- Metacognitive scaffolding improves calibration.
- Unrestricted AI increases overreliance.

Required support:

- At least one Tier 2 or Tier 3 source.
- Preprints may supplement but should not be sole support.

### Moderate Claims

Examples:

- LLM tutors may over-intervene.
- Scaffold timing is an important design variable.
- Immediate accuracy can diverge from durable learning.

Required support:

- Tier 1 plus Tier 2, Tier 3, or cautious Tier 4 support.

### Conceptual Claims

Examples:

- Scaffolds should be temporary.
- Productive struggle can support learning.
- Learners need metacognitive monitoring.

Required support:

- Tier 1 is acceptable, preferably with Tier 2 support where available.

### Context Claims

Examples:

- Educators are concerned about AI overreliance.
- Generative AI creates new instructional-design challenges.

Required support:

- Tier 5 is acceptable when clearly framed as context.

## Manuscript Practice

Each manuscript claim should be tagged during drafting as:

```text
[theory]
[empirical]
[systematic-review]
[preprint]
[context]
```

This prevents overclaiming and helps prepare the final citation audit.
