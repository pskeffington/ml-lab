# Citation Workflow

## Purpose

This workflow keeps the manuscript citation base accurate, cautious, and publication-ready.

## Step 1: Add Candidate Source

Add the source to `docs/literature_matrix.md` with:

- Source name
- Core idea
- Manuscript use
- Relation to the project

## Step 2: Draft APA Entry

Add a draft APA 7 entry to `docs/apa_sources.md`.

Required fields:

- Author names
- Year
- Title
- Venue or publisher
- DOI or stable URL when available
- Evidence type
- Manuscript use
- Priority

## Step 3: Assign Evidence Tier

Use `docs/evidence_quality_rubric.md` to classify the source.

Allowed tiers:

- Tier 1: Foundational theory
- Tier 2: Peer-reviewed empirical evidence
- Tier 3: Systematic review or meta-analysis
- Tier 4: Preprint or emerging system paper
- Tier 5: Policy, commentary, survey, or context source

## Step 4: Link Source to Manuscript Claim

Every important manuscript claim should be supported by at least one source in the claim-to-citation map.

Strong empirical claims require Tier 2 or Tier 3 support.

## Step 5: Citation Audit Before Submission

Before submitting the manuscript:

1. Verify every APA entry.
2. Check every DOI or stable URL.
3. Confirm whether preprints have peer-reviewed versions.
4. Remove sources that do not directly support a manuscript claim.
5. Ensure synthetic results are described as protocol-validation artifacts, not human-learning evidence.
